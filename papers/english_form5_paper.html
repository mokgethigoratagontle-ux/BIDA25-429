SECTION A: CONTINUOUS WRITING (50 Marks)
Write a composition of between 350 and 450 words on ONE of the following topics.
Topic 1 (Narrative)
Write a story that ends with the words: "...I looked back one last time and realized that my life would never be the same again." [50 Marks]
Topic 2 (Argumentative)
"Social media platforms have done more to isolate people than to bring them together." Do you agree? Write an essay supporting your viewpoint. [50 Marks]
Topic 3 (Descriptive)
Describe a local market in your town during its busiest hours. Focus on the sounds, smells, sights, and interactions among buyers and sellers. [50 Marks]
SECTION B: COMPREHENSION & SUMMARY (50 Marks)
Read the following passage carefully and answer the structured questions that follow.

[1] The sun rose like a gold coin tossed into a slate-colored sky, casting its first weak beams over the quiet village of Kgale. It was the morning of the great harvest, an event that occurred once every three years. For months, the villagers had worked the soil under a punishing drought, their calloused hands and aching backs a testament to their dedication. Now, the fields of golden sorghum waved under a gentle breeze, ready to reward their persistence.

[2] At the edge of the field stood Thabo, a young farmer whose family had cultivated this land for generations. His heart beat with a mixture of excitement and heavy anxiety. The drought had taken its toll, and this crop was their final hope. If the yield was poor, many families would have to leave their ancestral home for the crowded city streets in search of labor. He knelt down, running the rich, dry soil through his fingers, praying that the earth had been kind.

[3] As the village elders blew the traditional horn, indicating the start of the harvest, Thabo and his peers swung their scythes in perfect synchronization. The rhythmic sound of cutting stalks echoed through the valley. By mid-afternoon, the sweat ran freely, but no one slowed down. The communal spirit was alive; children carried cool water in clay pots, and elders sang traditional songs of encouragement. It was a beautiful display of unity that no drought could dry up.

Question 1
From Paragraph 1: Why was this particular harvest so significant to the villagers of Kgale? Give two reasons based on the text. [4 Marks]
Question 2
From Paragraph 2: Explain Thabo's mixed feelings on this morning. What was his greatest fear if the harvest failed? [4 Marks]
Question 3
From Paragraph 3: Identify a metaphor used by the writer to describe the community's resilience during the drought. [3 Marks]
Question 4 (Vocabulary)
Find words or phrases in the passage that have the same or similar meaning as:
(i) testament (Paragraph 1): ________________________
(ii) cultivated (Paragraph 2): ________________________
(iii) synchronization (Paragraph 3): ________________________
[3 Marks]
Question 5 (Summary Writing)
In not more than 80 words, summarize the ways in which the community showed unity and supported each other during the day of the harvest, as described in Paragraph 3. Write your summary in a single continuous paragraph. [10 Marks]
