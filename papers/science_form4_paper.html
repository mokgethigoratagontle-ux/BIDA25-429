Form 4 Practice Examination Paper
INTEGRATED SCIENCE
Time Allowed: 2 Hours Total Marks: 100

Instructions to Candidates:

  • Write your full name in the spaces provided at the top of this sheet.
  • Answer ALL questions in this paper.
  • Write your answers and descriptions clearly in the space provided.
  • Drawing diagrams where appropriate is encouraged.
  • All calculations must show relevant formulas and units.
Question 1 (Biology)
Cells are the fundamental units of all living organisms. Compare and contrast plant and animal cells.
(a) List three structural organelles found in plant cells that are absent in animal cells. [3 Marks]
(b) Describe the primary function of the mitochondrion and state why it is commonly referred to as the "powerhouse" of the cell. [5 Marks]
Question 2 (Chemistry)
An atom of Sodium (Na) has an atomic number of 11 and a mass number of 23.
(a) State the number of protons, neutrons, and electrons in a neutral Sodium atom. [3 Marks]
(b) Write the electron configuration (electronic arrangement) of Sodium, and draw a simple Bohr model diagram showing the distribution of its electrons in shells. [5 Marks]
Question 3 (Physics)
A car of mass 1200 kg is travelling at a constant velocity of 15 m/s. The driver hits the brakes, bringing the car to a complete stop over a distance of 45 meters.
(a) Calculate the deceleration (retardation) of the car, showing your equation. [4 Marks]
(b) Calculate the average braking force exerted on the car to bring it to a halt. (Hint: Use F = ma). [4 Marks]
Question 4 (Chemistry/Biology)
Photosynthesis is a vital biological process that occurs in green plants.
(a) Write down the balanced chemical equation representing photosynthesis. [4 Marks]
(b) State the role of chlorophyll and sunlight energy in this process. [4 Marks]
Question 5 (Physics/Environment)
Energy is neither created nor destroyed, but transformed from one form to another.
(a) Explain this statement in reference to the Law of Conservation of Energy. [3 Marks]
(b) A hydroelectric power station utilizes falling water to generate electricity. Describe the chain of energy conversions that take place, starting from the water behind the dam to the electricity produced. [5 Marks]
